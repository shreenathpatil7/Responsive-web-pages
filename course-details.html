Website Design

Imagine your website as a captivating story waiting to unfold. HTML acts as the storyteller, laying out the plot and introducing each character. It structures the narrative, ensuring a logical flow from one scene to the next. Each page is a chapter, and HTML is the author weaving the tale. CSS, on the other hand, is the stylistic maestro, adding color, shaping the characters' appearances, and setting the mood. It's the artist who paints the scenes, choosing the right fonts and colors to convey the emotions of your story. Together, HTML and CSS create an immersive experience, inviting visitors to become engrossed in the narrative of your website. Just like a well-crafted story, your website's design should capture attention, evoke emotions, and leave a lasting impression. It's the perfect blend of structure and style, where every element plays a crucial role in the overall storytelling experience.

Introduction to HTML

HTML, or Hypertext Markup Language, is like the foundation of the webâ€”it's the language that structures the content on a webpage. Think of it as the backbone, providing a framework for all the text, images, links, and other elements you see on your favorite sites.

In simpler terms, HTML uses a series of tags (enclosed in angle brackets like tags) to define the different parts of a document. For example, h1 tags indicate a top-level heading, p tags represent paragraphs, and img tags bring images to life.

Basics of HTML and CSS

HTML is the structural architect of the web. It uses a series of tags to define and organize content on a webpage

CSS (Cascading Style Sheets): CSS is the designer and artist of the web. While HTML structures the content, CSS enhances its appearance. With CSS, you can control colors, fonts, spacing, and layout. It allows you to transform a plain webpage into a visually stunning and user-friendly experience.
